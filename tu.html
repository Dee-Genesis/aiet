Student Refund Policy

AIET is committed to fairness and transparency in all financial dealings with students. The following refund policy applies to all program enrollments and governs the circumstances under which refunds may be issued.

5.1 Refund Schedule

Refund eligibility is determined by when the withdrawal or refund request is made relative to the date of enrollment and course access:

5.2 How to Request a Refund

All refund requests must be submitted in writing to AIET via email at [email protected]. Requests must include:

  • Full name and enrollment details
  • Program name and enrollment date
  • Reason for refund request
  • Proof of payment (where applicable)

Approved refunds will be processed within 10–15 working days from the date the refund is confirmed. Refunds are issued via the original payment method where possible.

5.5 Program Deferral Alternative

Before requesting a refund, students are encouraged to contact AIET to explore the option of deferring their enrollment to a future intake. Deferral requests are considered on a case-by-case basis and may be a more suitable option for students facing temporary personal or professional challenges.

Student Code of Conduct

All students enrolled in AIET programs are expected to conduct themselves with professionalism, respect, and integrity at all times. The following standards of conduct apply across all interactions within the AIET learning environment, including online platforms, communications with instructors, and engagement with fellow students.

6.1 Expected Behavior

  • Treat all instructors, staff, and fellow students with courtesy and respect
  • Participate in academic activities honestly and in good faith
  • Comply with all institutional policies, guidelines, and instructions issued by AIET
  • Maintain the confidentiality of other students' personal information
  • Report any concerns or grievances through appropriate channels

6.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action, suspension, or dismissal:

  • Harassment, bullying, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
  • Fraudulent representation of identity or qualifications
  • Unauthorized sharing or distribution of AIET course materials
  • Disruption of the learning environment or academic activities
  • Any conduct that brings AIET into disrepute
⚠ Disciplinary Action: Violations of the Student Code of Conduct may result in formal warnings, suspension of access, dismissal from the program, and/or forfeiture of all fees paid. In serious cases, AIET may report conduct to relevant authorities.

Academic Integrity

AIET places the highest value on academic honesty and the integrity of its qualifications. All work submitted by students must be their own original work, unless collaboration is explicitly authorized.

7.1 Prohibited Academic Practices

  • Plagiarism: Presenting another person's work, ideas, or language as your own without proper attribution
  • Contract Cheating: Submitting work produced wholly or partially by another person, AI tool, or third-party service as your own
  • Collusion: Working with others without authorization on individually assessed tasks
  • Fabrication: Inventing or falsifying data, research findings, or citations
  • Certificate Fraud: Falsifying, altering, or misrepresenting any AIET certificate or academic record

7.2 Consequences

Academic misconduct may result in any or all of the following:

  • Automatic failure of the relevant assessment or module
  • Dismissal from the program without refund
  • Permanent academic record notation
  • Revocation of any certificate already issued
  • Reporting to relevant professional or regulatory bodies
